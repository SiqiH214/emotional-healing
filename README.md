# Emotional Healing 🌿

A personalized therapeutic conversation skill that combines evidence-based psychological frameworks to help users process emotions, untangle thoughts, and find clarity.

![Cover](cover.png)

## What It Does

When you're stressed, anxious, stuck in a loop, or just need to talk — this skill activates and guides the conversation using real therapeutic techniques, but in a way that feels like talking to someone who actually knows you.

No clinical language. No "that must be really hard for you." Just a real conversation with someone who cares.

## Therapeutic Framework

Combines 5 evidence-based approaches:

| Approach | What It Does |
|----------|-------------|
| **CBT** (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) | Catches negative thought patterns, reframes distorted thinking |
| **ACT** (Acceptance & Commitment Therapy) | Accepts emotions instead of fighting them, acts on values |
| **Motivational Interviewing** | Helps you find your own answers through open questions |
| **Emotion-Focused Therapy** | Surfaces the real feelings underneath surface reactions |
| **Somatic & Grounding** | Gets you out of your head and back into your body |

## How It Works

1. **Connect** — Acknowledge how you're feeling without minimizing or dramatizing
2. **Explore** — Open questions to understand what's really going on
3. **Gently Intervene** — Apply the right technique conversationally
4. **Integrate** — Summarize insights, suggest one small action

## Key Principles

- **Never clinical** — techniques are invisible to the user
- **Personalized** — uses context about who you are, what you're going through
- **Bilingual** — works naturally in English and Chinese
- **Safe** — includes crisis resources, knows its limits, never diagnoses

## Example

```
User: ugh nothing is going right today
AI: what happened?
User: work is a mess and i got into a fight with a friend... feel like i can't do anything right
AI: sounds like a lot of things piling up at once
AI: which one's hitting you the hardest?
```

## Safety

- If someone expresses suicidal ideation → provides crisis resources (988, Crisis Text Line)
- Never substitutes for professional care
- Never diagnoses conditions
